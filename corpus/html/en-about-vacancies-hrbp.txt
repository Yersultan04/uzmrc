HR BP Responsibilities

Strategic Interaction with Management:

Consulting department heads on personnel management and business process optimization.

Participating in the development and implementation of the company's HR strategy.

Analyzing current staffing needs, forecasting future requirements, and developing appropriate solutions.

Contracts and Agreements:

Overseeing the conclusion, amendment, and termination of employment contracts.

Developing and approving standard forms of civil law contracts (CLC).

Coordinating with the legal department on labor relations and CLC legal support.

Monitoring compliance with contract terms (both labor and CLC) and assessing risks associated with their violation.

Personnel Management:

Developing and implementing employee training, motivation, and development programs.

Handling complex labor relations issues, including disputes and conflicts.

Interacting with employee representatives.

Recruitment:

Developing and implementing hiring strategies.

Conducting selection, interviews, and onboarding of new employees.

Coordinating recruitment needs with department heads.

Analytics and Reporting:

Maintaining analytics on key HR indicators: staff turnover, employee satisfaction levels, training effectiveness, etc.

Preparing management reports on personnel management.

Compliance with Legislation:

Ensuring compliance with labor laws, including requirements for employment contracts and CLCs.

Providing consultations on labor and civil law for internal stakeholders.

Working Conditions:

Schedule: 5/2, standard working hours.

Salary: Competitive (discussed during the interview).

Additional Benefits:

Compensation for training and professional development.

Reimbursement for gym membership.

Voluntary medical insurance.

Team-building events and corporate activities.
